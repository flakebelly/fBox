Cookie Policy

fBox Audio — fboxaudio.com

Last updated: March 2026

The short version: fboxaudio.com does not use advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or any tracking. The only cookies present are strictly necessary technical cookies set by Cloudflare for security purposes.

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They serve various purposes from keeping you logged in to tracking your behaviour across the web.

fBox Audio has no interest in tracking you. We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other tracking or advertising technology.

Cookies We Use

The only cookies on fboxaudio.com are set by Cloudflare, our website security and performance provider. These are strictly necessary cookies — they exist to protect the website and its visitors from security threats and cannot be disabled without breaking core site functionality.

These cookies do not track you across other websites, are not used for advertising, and contain no personally identifiable information beyond what is necessary for their security function.

Third Party Cookies

When you visit our store or make a purchase, you may be directed to a third-party payment processor. Their own cookie policy will apply on their platform. We have no control over third-party cookies set outside of fboxaudio.com.

No Cookie Banner

Because fboxaudio.com uses only strictly necessary cookies, we are not required to display a cookie consent banner. Strictly necessary cookies are exempt from consent requirements under UK PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations).

Managing Cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to view, delete, and block cookies. Note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may affect the security and performance of the website.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever introduce new cookies — for example if we add analytics in future — we will update this policy and add a consent mechanism if required. We will not add tracking or advertising cookies without clearly communicating this.
